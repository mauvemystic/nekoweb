Movies I've Seen

Note: I rarely watch a movie of my own accord, AKA people have to drag me out to the theatre or pop something on for me to watch a movie. I don't dislike film, it's just not my preferred medium, I suppose. Keep this context in mind when reading my "reviews."

September 2025

Straw (2025)

My friend chose the movie for us, and he swears he did not notice Tyler Perry was the producer. I have never watched any of his movies, and I see why. I understood where he was *trying* to go with this story but I do not understand why the store manager had to be killed. I am the empathy queen, I know that people are often in circumstances where they feel forced to act in ways they wouldn't otherwise. The story would have made more sense if the store manager wasn't murdered, and I would have given this a 2.5 or 3 stars (all other plotholes and questionable writing aside).

Genre: Drama
Most enjoyed: Teyana Taylor and Taraji P. Hensen made this bearable and carried the movie, as I expected.

Personal rating:

August 2025

EDEN (2025)

I like a good spy-themed "who dunnit" movie, and this movie doesn't disappoint there. There's a decent mix of suspense, drama, and comedy, which I enjoyed. I did feel like it was missing some bigger moment or climax; I can't put my finger on why I didn't enjoy this as much as I wanted to.

Genre: Suspense / Drama
Most enjoyed: Marisa Abela's performance. I've not seen her in anything before, and I'd love to see more of her!

Personal rating:

April 2025

The Minecraft Movie (2025)

  • Good run time, 100 minutes was alright enough to tell the story
  • Danielle Brooks’ part was not fleshed out at all and that sucks, her character felt more like an archetype than an actual person and there was no way to get attached
  • I love Jack Black in this, he really got me through the beginning before the story and action picked up
  • I liked Jason Momoa in this as well (surprisingly), the comedic pairing with Black was better than I expected
  • I went into this completely blind so I had no clue Jennifer Coolidge would make an appearance, the movie is 200% better for it
  • The song choices were not great, it really stuck out to me that they used “Lil Boo Thang” by Paul Russell during the first real jump in the plot and first actual piece of action; I know nothing about movies but I guess they really phoned that part in and budgeted money elsewhere
  • I have feelings about the sentiment “being an adult sucks” because it’s not the growing up that inherently sucks but the systems that people are forced to grow into; I understand this is for families though so I understand why they went with the theme of bringing childlike creativity and passion into the “adult” world to create the world you want to live in
  • I love minecraft so I enjoyed the movie overall for what it was, I had a good time getting to see minecraft brought to “real life” and it was even more enjoyable with my kids. I'm sure my score is heavily influenced by that.

Genre: Family / Comedy / Fantasy
Most enjoyed: Jennifer Coolidge's appearances, hands down

Personal rating:

Bull Moon Rising

I appreciated that the actual plot didn't hinge so much on the romance that I couldn't get into it -- I found the problems Aspeth, the protagonist, ran into compelling. While the ending was unsurprising, it was a pleasure to see just how she was going to get there. I haven't read any fantasy or romance in a very long while, and it was a joy to remember how fun fiction can be. Not a slow-starter, which made it easy to get into.

The physical book itself is GORGEOUS, especially the edge printing. The end is a bit anti-climatic, to be so honest with you. With the politics of the novel being set up and explained led me to believe there'd be more plot movement a la Games-of-Thrones-style. The whole reason Aspeth embarks on the reason for this book is because of her ties to the political powers that be, but I picked up this book because of how pretty it was. It was only once I got into the thick of it that I realized the focus would be less on the politics and more on the sexual frustrations between a beast and human. And it feels so... silly that everything gets wrapped up as smoothly as it does.

I do hope for a sequel, though. I'd like to see where Aspeth's story goes from here and how The Five's journey with the guild goes now that they're Repeaters. I'm also hoping for more on the aftermath of Magpie. Being the first and only woman in the guild ever (until Aspeth and Gwenn came along), we did not get nearly as much detail about her experiences as I would've liked. I suppose it wasn't a bad choice, though, to leave some mystery to her to flesh out later.

Author: Ruby Dixon
Genre: Fantasy romance
Released: 2024

April 2025

49 Days

I don't have much personal experience with death, and I wasn't sure what I was in for when I picked up the book. I found myself on a journey with a soul after passing and seeing how their relatives grieve and move forward as they can. There were times I felt a bit lost in what was happening. I imagine grieving (and perhaps even passing from form) feels a similar way. I appreciated the parallels made between the paths of the living and the path of those no longer in form. I had to be careful not to read to quickly, with very few words between the pages.
